# Code of Conduct

M2Suite exists to keep three-decade-old software readable. That work depends
on people sharing findings freely, including half-finished ones and wrong
ones — so the standard here is simple: **make it easy for someone to be
wrong in public.**

## What's expected

- **Be direct about the work, generous about the person.** "This offset is
  wrong, here's the file that disproves it" is exactly right. "You clearly
  didn't test this" is not.
- **Say how confident you are.** "I think" and "I verified" are different
  claims. Nobody is penalised for a labelled guess; unlabelled guesses cost
  everyone time.
- **Being corrected is normal here.** Several entries in
  [docs/FORMATS.md](docs/FORMATS.md) are corrections of things this project
  was confidently wrong about for a long time. A contributor who disproves
  something has done the most valuable kind of work, and should be thanked
  for it, not defended against.
- **Assume good faith about experience level.** People come to preservation
  from wildly different backgrounds. A question that seems basic is often
  someone approaching from a direction nobody here has tried.
- **Respect the no-game-data rule.** It protects the project and everyone in
  it. See [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md).

## What's not acceptable

- Harassment, personal attacks, or demeaning comments — including about
  someone's nationality, gender, sexuality, disability, religion,
  appearance, age, or level of experience
- Sexualised language or imagery, or unwelcome personal attention
- Publishing others' private information without permission
- Sustained disruption: derailing threads, dismissing others' work without
  substance, or reopening settled decisions to relitigate them

## Scope

This applies in issues, pull requests, discussions, commit messages, and
anywhere someone is representing the project.

## Reporting

Report problems by opening an issue, or privately to the maintainer if the
issue involves someone's conduct and shouldn't be public.

Reports are handled in confidence. The maintainer will respond, and may
edit or remove content, or block accounts, where that's warranted. Nobody
will be penalised for reporting in good faith.

## A note on this document

This project is small. There's no committee and no process theatre — the
maintainer decides. This document exists so expectations are written down
rather than assumed, which matters most for the person who hasn't
contributed yet and is trying to work out whether they'd be welcome.

You would be.
